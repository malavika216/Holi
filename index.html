Welcome to the Holi Festival!

Holi is one of the most vibrant festivals celebrated in India. It is known as the Festival of Colors and love.

About Holi

Holi is a Hindu festival celebrated at the end of winter. It marks the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. It usually takes place in March.

The night before Holi, people light a bonfire called Holika Dahan to celebrate the burning of evil.

How Holi is Celebrated

People throw colored powders and water at each other, dance to music, and enjoy sweets like Gujiya and Thandai.

Holi spreads joy, unity, and friendship across all ages and communities.
