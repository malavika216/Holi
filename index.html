Welcome to the Holi Festival!

Holi is one of the most vibrant and colorful festivals celebrated in India. It marks the arrival of spring and the end of winter. People come together to play with powders, dance to music, and share delicious sweets. This joyful festival breaks social barriers and promotes unity across communities.

About Holi

Holi has its roots in ancient Hindu legends like that of Prahlad and Holika. The night before the festival, bonfires are lit in a ritual called Holika Dahan to symbolize the victory of good over evil. It also celebrates the playful love of Radha and Krishna, whose colorful pranks inspire the tradition. Today, Holi is celebrated not only in India but across the world, bringing people of diverse backgrounds together.

How Holi is Celebrated

On the day of Holi, streets come alive as people smear each other with vibrant powders and colored water. Traditional foods like gujiya and beverages like thandai add flavor to the festivities. Many communities now use eco-friendly colors to protect skin and the environment. Music, dance, and street performances transform public spaces into lively celebrations of joy and harmony.
